# 12 Virtues

[[Twelve Virtues of Rationality|**Twelve Virtues of Rationality**]] is a short essay written by [Eliezer Yudkowsky](https://www.baserates.org/users/eliezer_yudkowsky). The twelve virtues are [[Curiosity|curiosity]], [relinquishment](https://wiki.lesswrong.com/wiki/relinquishment), [lightness](https://wiki.lesswrong.com/wiki/lightness), [[Evenness|evenness]], [argument](https://wiki.lesswrong.com/wiki/argument), [[Empiricism|empiricism]], [simplicity](https://wiki.lesswrong.com/wiki/simplicity), [[Humility|humility]], [[Perfectionism|perfectionism]],  [precision](https://wiki.lesswrong.com/wiki/precision),  [[Scholarship & Learning|scholarship]], and a [twelfth virtue](https://www.lesswrong.com/tag/twelfth-virtue) that is nameless, or called "the void."
