# Carl Shulman

**Carl Matthew Shulman** (born ca. 1982) is a Canadian-American researcher. He is currently a Research Associate at the [[Future of Humanity Institute]] and an Advisor to [[Open Philanthropy]]. Previously, he was a Research Fellow at the [[Machine Intelligence Research Institute]] and Director of Careers Research at [[80,000 Hours]]. He also worked at Clarium Capital, an investment management and hedge fund company, and at Reed Smith LLP, a global law firm. Shulman attended New York University School of Law and holds a BA in philosophy from Harvard University.

Shulman has made substantive contributions to several areas, including [[Career choice|career choice]], [[Immigration reform|open borders]], [[Wild animal welfare|wild animal welfare]], [[EA/Topics/Existential risk|existential risk]], [[Decision theory|decision theory]], [[Whole brain emulation|whole brain emulation]], [[Earning to give|earning to give]], among many others. Concepts and ideas original to him include [[Iterated embryo selection|iterated embryo selection]], [[Hedonium|hedonium]], [[Donor lotteries|donor lotteries]], and others.

Shulman administers a $5 million discretionary fund held by the [[Centre for Effective Altruism]], funded by an [[Open Philanthropy]] grant.^[\[1\]](#fnyltpattrxsk)^
