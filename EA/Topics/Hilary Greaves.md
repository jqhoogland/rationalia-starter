# Hilary Greaves

**Hilary Greaves** (born 29 September 1978) is a British philosopher. She is currently a professor at the University of Oxford and the director of the [[Global Priorities Institute]].

Greaves earned a BA in philosophy and physics from Oxford and a PhD in philosophy from Rutgers. Her doctoral thesis, completed under the supervision of Frank Arntzenius, was "Spacetime symmetries and the CPT theorem".^[\[1\]](#fn2u3b4m45miq)^ Prior to her current professorship, she held appointments at Merton and Somerville Colleges.

Greaves’ current work is on issues related to [[Global priorities research|global priorities research]]. Her research interests include [[Moral philosophy|moral philosophy]] (including foundational issues in [[Consequentialism|consequentialism]], interpersonal aggregation, [[Population ethics|population ethics]], and [[Moral uncertainty|moral uncertainty]]), formal epistemology, and the philosophy of physics.
