# Stanford Existential Risks Initiative

**Stanford Existential Risks Initiative** (**SERI**) is a collaboration between faculty and students of Stanford University dedicated to mitigating [[Global catastrophic risk|global catastrophic risks]].^[\[1\]](#fnbq88yj5f5ub)^ It was launched on 15 May 2020.^[\[2\]](#fnqc1jrnl0it)^

SERI runs an undergraduate summer research program, various speaker events and discussions, and a class by Professors Stephen Luby and Paul Edwards on "preventing human extinction".^[\[3\]](#fn6z56wl5toeu)^ 

Funding
-------

As of June 2022, SERI has received over $1.5 million in funding from [[Open Philanthropy]],^[\[4\]](#fn8dvkb3rbg0w)^ and $125,000 from the [[Survival and Flourishing]] Fund,^[\[5\]](#fn3xm29jmbq9l)^ and $60,000 from [[Effective Altruism Funds]].^[\[6\]](#fnyod65gee00p)^
