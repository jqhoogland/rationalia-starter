Cached Thoughts are ideas, attitudes, and beliefs that a person has formed on some past occasion, and hasn't re-evaluated since then. The name references the concept of a [cache](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cache_(computing)) in computing: a component storing data that has been calculated or retrieved once, so that it is quickly available without needing to be recalculated or re-retrieved.

*See also*: [[Groupthink]], [[Information Cascades]], [[Status Quo Bias|Status quo bias]], [[Semantic Stopsign]], [[Bad title|Separate Magisteria]]

Cached thoughts can be useful in saving computational resources at the cost of some memory load, and also at the risk of maintaining a belief long past the point when evidence should force an update. In particular, cached thoughts can result in a lack of creative approaches to problem-solving, as cached solutions may interfere with the formation of novel ones.
