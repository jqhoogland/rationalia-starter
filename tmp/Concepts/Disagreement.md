# Disagreement
Disagreement is when two people have different beliefs.

## 
Considered of particular relevance to disagreement between people trying to be rational, [Aumann's agreement theorem](https://lessestwrong.com/tag/aumann-s-agreement-theorem) can be [[Aumann Agreement|informally interpreted]] as suggesting that if two people are honest seekers of truth, and both *believe* each other to be honest, then they should update on each other's opinions and quickly reach agreement. The very fact that a person believes something is [[1 Projects/Learning/Rationalism/Concepts/Rational Evidence|Rational evidence]] that that something is true, and so this fact [should be taken into account](http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/01/extraordinary_c.html) when forming your belief.

Outside of well-functioning [[Prediction Markets|prediction markets]], Aumann agreement can probably only be approximated by careful deliberative discourse.
